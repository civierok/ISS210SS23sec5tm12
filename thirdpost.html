The Impact of Poverty on Educational Outcomes for Children

How poverty impacts the learning ability of children

Posted by Michael Hamilton The Impact of Poverty on Educational Outcomes For Children on April 28, 2023

The income gaps in rich countries, like Canada, have been widening, and it affects the efficiency of children in schools in the past decade. This hurts families because children's educational outcome is a huge determiner for financial survival.

The Ontario Child Health Study in the 80s states that there are associations between low income and mental illness, and chronic physical health problems in children. When children don't have the proper financial standing they can't get the medicine they need. With the financial stress, it affects their mental health, which affects their academics.

Poverty hurts a child's school readiness because of their home life which is hurt by poverty, along with health, and the neighborhoods they live in. When a child doesn't get the right amount of stimulation from their home life, it's hard for them to focus on school and develope proper social skills.

According to The Institution of Research and Public Policy, children who are impoverished are less likely to pass standard tests, and they also score lower on vocabulary tests.

Children in poverty are set up for failure. They're not given the stable home life to succeed. They don't have the physical health to be present in classes, and neither do they have the mental health. Many students may be in class physically, but are they really present? Every child in every country should be given the opportunity to academically grow with one another.

The Effects

Poverty effects children in a lot more ways than just their education. They can feel left out, they could be picked on for what they're wearing, their worries could be focused on things that children shouldn't be stressed about too. They can have low self esteem and low self worth. Children in poverty also miss out on a lot of childhood experiences, like going on field trips. Going on field trips often times costs money. Money the parents don't have, so the children end up losing an opportunity. All of these things effect children heavily. Growing up like this makes it harder to be a functioning adult, too. The longterm effects poverty has on youth takes a life-time to heal from.
